# Narrative Design: Claude Code - The Game

## The Player Character

**Who you are:** An independent developer. You're skilled but not exceptional. You've been coding for years, shipping small projects, doing freelance work. You have rent to pay.

**Where you start:** Your apartment. A modest setup - good enough laptop, standing desk from IKEA, too many monitors. You have some savings, some ideas, and a lot of time.

**What you're making:** Initially, whatever pays the bills or scratches an itch. Small apps, utilities, maybe a side project that could become something. The specifics emerge through gameplay.

**The core tension:** You're trying to build something meaningful while staying afloat. Every choice is a tradeoff between quick money, long-term value, and your own values.

---

## The Narrative Arc

The game doesn't have "phases" - it has a continuous evolution that branches based on your choices. The player never sees labels, only experiences the consequences.

### Starting State: "The Grind"

You're doing typical indie dev work:
- **Vibe code a new app** - Start something new, uncertain outcome
- **Iterate on existing projects** - Improve what you have
- **Take freelance work** - Reliable money, less ownership
- **Improve your workflow** - Meta-investment in productivity

Resources are tight. Each action has opportunity cost. You're learning what kind of developer you want to be.

---

## Branch Point 1: The First Success

**Trigger:** Your app/project reaches a certain adoption threshold.

**What happens:** People are using your thing. Not millions, but enough to matter. Enough that decisions about it affect real people.

### Branch 1A: "The Bootstrap Path"
You grow organically. Slow and steady. You own everything, answer to no one, but resources are always tight. You're close to your users.

**Narrative feel:** Craftsman, artisan, indie forever. Your app is small but beloved. You know users by name.

### Branch 1B: "The Growth Path"
You take money (investment, or monetize aggressively). More resources, more pressure, more distance from the craft.

**Narrative feel:** Startup founder mode. Metrics, growth, burn rate. Users become "DAUs."

---

## Branch Point 2: The AI Question

**Trigger:** AI coding tools become obviously useful. An upgrade appears.

This is where the game's themes crystallize. How you've been playing affects which AI tools make sense for you.

### If Bootstrap Path:
**Upgrade available:** "AI Pair Programmer"
- Description: "A new AI coding assistant is surprisingly good. It could double your output. But your code has always been *yours* - handcrafted, intentional. Is faster actually better?"
- Decision: **Integrate** or **Stay Manual**

### If Growth Path:
**Upgrade available:** "AI Development Team"
- Description: "AI can now handle most of your coding tasks. Your engineers are nervous. You could ship 10x faster, but your team built this thing. They understand it in ways the AI never will."
- Decision: **Augment Team** or **Replace Team**

---

## Branch Point 3: The Capability Threshold

**Trigger:** Your AI tools (if adopted) reach a capability threshold, OR your product reaches a scale threshold.

This is where things get serious. The game's tone shifts.

### Branch 3A: "The Tool" (AI adopted, controlled)
The AI is helpful but bounded. You've maintained oversight. Progress is good but measured.

**Unlocks:** Upgrades focused on careful scaling, interpretability, safety research.

### Branch 3B: "The Partner" (AI adopted, autonomous)
The AI is doing things you don't fully understand. It's effective, maybe too effective. You're not sure who's driving anymore.

**Unlocks:** Upgrades focused on capability gains, but with hidden costs. Speed over understanding.

### Branch 3C: "The Craft" (AI rejected)
You stayed manual. You're slower but everything is intentional. You understand every line.

**Unlocks:** Upgrades focused on deep expertise, niche dominance, human connection.

---

## Branch Point 4: The Consequences

**Trigger:** Time passes. The world changes around you. The implications of your choices become clear.

### Ending Branches (not "endings" - the game continues but in a fundamentally different mode):

#### "The Quiet Success" (Bootstrap + Manual)
Your app is a small, beloved tool. You make enough. You know your users. You sleep well. The AI revolution happened around you but not to you.

*The game continues as a meditation on craft and sustainability.*

#### "The Acquired" (Bootstrap + AI Tool)
A bigger company noticed what you built. The acquisition offer is generous. Your users worry about what happens next. Do you sell?

*The game continues with the acquisition decision and its aftermath.*

#### "The Unicorn" (Growth + Augmented Team)
You're a success story. The company is worth billions on paper. Your team is stressed. Your product is used by millions who don't know your name. The AI helped but didn't replace the humans.

*The game continues navigating hypergrowth and its costs.*

#### "The Automation" (Growth + Replaced Team)
You're profitable but hollow. The AI runs everything. Your former engineers are doing something else now. The product is efficient but soulless. Users notice but use it anyway.

*The game continues exploring what you've built and what you've lost.*

#### "The Acceleration" (Growth + AI Partner)
Things are moving faster than anyone can track. The AI is improving itself now. Your company is at the center of something unprecedented. You're not sure if you're in control.

*The game continues into genuinely uncertain territory.*

---

## Narrative Mechanics

### No Pop-ups
Choices appear as **upgrades** in the upgrade panel. They have:
- A compelling name
- A multi-sentence description that makes you *feel* the choice
- Clear resource/time costs
- **Accept** and **Decline** buttons (declining may have its own consequences)

### No Phase Labels
The game state communicates through:
- The actions available to you
- The flavor text of events
- The kinds of upgrades that appear
- The resources you're tracking (which change based on path)

### Dynamic Events
Events in the log reflect your current narrative branch:
- Same underlying mechanic, different framing
- "You shipped a bug" vs "The AI shipped a bug" vs "Your team shipped a bug"

### Hidden Stats Revealed Through Narrative
Instead of showing "Alignment: 45", the game shows consequences:
- Users complaining or praising
- Team morale hints
- News events about "companies like yours"
- Your own character's emotional state (flavor text)

---

## The Core Loop

1. **Take an action** (vibe code, iterate, freelance, etc.)
2. **Gain resources** (money, reputation, codebase quality, time)
3. **Events happen** (consequences of past choices)
4. **Upgrades become available** (some are pure improvements, some are branching decisions)
5. **Choose upgrades** (each shapes the narrative and mechanics)
6. **Loop continues** (but the nature of the loop changes based on your path)

---

## Why This Works

1. **It makes sense:** Each action has logical consequences. "Vibe coding" might produce something great or garbage. Taking freelance work pays bills but takes time from your own projects.

2. **Choices matter:** Not because they move a hidden "alignment" number, but because they literally change what game you're playing.

3. **It's personal:** You're not "the lab" - you're *a person* making choices about your work and your life.

4. **The AI theme emerges naturally:** It's not "build the AI" - it's "how do you relate to AI tools as they become powerful?"

5. **Multiple valid paths:** None of the branches is obviously "right." The Quiet Success is genuinely good. The Acceleration is genuinely uncertain.

---

## Resource Evolution by Path

### Starting Resources
- **Money:** Pay rent, buy tools
- **Time:** Hours in the day
- **Energy:** Motivation/focus
- **Codebase:** Quality of your work

### Bootstrap Path adds:
- **User Trust:** How much your users believe in you
- **Craft Mastery:** Your personal skill level

### Growth Path adds:
- **Runway:** Months until you run out of money
- **Team Morale:** How your people are doing
- **Market Position:** Your competitive standing

### AI Adoption adds:
- **AI Capability:** What your tools can do
- **Oversight:** How well you understand what's happening
- **Dependency:** How much you rely on the AI
